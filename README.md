#Project Overview :-
The Digital Literacy Awareness Project is an essential educational initiative designed to transform first-year students into responsible digital citizens. As a Student Digital Ambassador, your mission is to lead by example, helping your peers navigate the online world with professional confidence and safety. By building this portfolio, you demonstrate your ability to create professional identities, use collaborative platforms efficiently, and maintain high standards of digital ethics. This project is not just a requirement; it is a foundation for your professional and personal growth in a digital-first world.

Through five structured tasks, you will develop a diverse set of digital skills—ranging from graphic design and professional networking to coding and cybersecurity awareness. Each part of this repository represents a milestone in mastering the tools needed for modern academic and career success. By documenting your progress, you create a permanent digital asset that proves your technical awareness and your commitment to responsible online behavior.


## Project Modules
# -Module 1: Creating a Digital Awareness Poster
In this task, students use creative tools like Canva or Prezi to design an infographic that explains the importance of digital literacy. As an Ambassador, you create a visual resource that covers key topics like safe internet habits or useful digital tools for students. This helps simplify complex information into an easy-to-understand guide for your classmates.

# -Module 2: Setting Up Professional Profiles
This module focuses on building a professional "Digital Footprint" by setting up accounts on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. Students learn how to present their education and skills in a way that attracts employers and the academic community. It marks the transition from using the internet for entertainment to using it as a tool for career networking.

# -Module 3: Learning to Code and Collaborate
This task introduces the power of cloud collaboration and technical practice. Students complete a beginner coding challenge on platforms like HackerRank or CodeChef and create a functional "Digital Literacy Quiz" using Google Forms. These skills are vital for working in teams and solving problems in a modern workplace.

# -Module 4: Professional Emails and Good Manners
Communication is key to professional success, so this module focuses on mastering email etiquette and social media behavior. Students practice writing formal emails to professors and industry professionals while creating a "Do’s and Don’ts" checklist for social media. This ensures your online interactions always remain respectful and professional.

# -Module 5: Staying Safe from Online Scams
The final module is dedicated to cybersecurity awareness and protecting personal data. Students research real-world threats like phishing or UPI fraud and create a step-by-step guide on how to stay safe. It also highlights essential resources like the National Cyber Crime Portal to ensure everyone knows how to report a crime.
