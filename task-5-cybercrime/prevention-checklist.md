# PREVENTION CHECKLIST

## *Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for Indian College Students*

•	Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money: A PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone tells you to "scan and enter PIN" to receive a refund or prize, it is a scam.

•	Verify UPI IDs before clicking 'Pay': Scammers often create fake handles that look like official college or utility accounts. 

* Always double-check the recipient's name in the UPI app before confirming.

•	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your WhatsApp, Instagram, and Gmail accounts. This ensures that even if someone gets your password, they cannot access your account without the secondary code.

•	Avoid "Free Campus Wi-Fi" for sensitive tasks: Never log into bank accounts or enter credit card details while on unencrypted public networks. Use your mobile data for any transaction or login.

•	Beware of "Part-Time Job" or "Scholarship" links: If you receive a WhatsApp message offering high pay for simple tasks (like liking videos), it is likely a phishing attempt to steal your data or money.

•	Audit your Social Media Privacy: Set your profiles to private and avoid sharing photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or Aadhaar card, as these contain sensitive data used for identity theft.

•	Keep your Software Updated: Always install the latest security patches for your OS and apps. These updates often fix "zero-day" vulnerabilities that hackers use to gain remote access.

•	Stick to Official App Stores: Avoid downloading "Modded" versions of apps (like GB WhatsApp or cracked games) from third-party sites, as these often contain hidden spyware.

* Report Cybercrime Immediately: Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the National Helpline at 1930.
